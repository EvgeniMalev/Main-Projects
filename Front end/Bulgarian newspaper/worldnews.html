World Health Organization Declares End to Polio Epidemic After Decades of Efforts"

After decades of relentless global efforts to eradicate polio, the World Health Organization (WHO) has declared the end of the polio epidemic, marking a historic victory in the fight against infectious diseases. This monumental achievement is the result of coordinated vaccination campaigns, surveillance systems, and public health initiatives implemented worldwide. The declaration signifies a major milestone in public health, as polio becomes only the second human disease eradicated through vaccination efforts, following smallpox. However, while celebrating this success, health officials emphasize the importance of ongoing vigilance to prevent the resurgence of polio and ensure global immunization efforts remain robust.
