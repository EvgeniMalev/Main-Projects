Mayor the head of the town

The Mayor position in Bulgaria holds immense significance, acting as a vital pillar in the country's governance structure. Historically, this role has been pivotal in shaping the trajectory of Bulgaria's cities and municipalities, influencing policies that directly impact the lives of its citizens. The Mayor serves as a bridge between the local government and the community, entrusted with the responsibility of addressing pressing issues such as urban development, infrastructure, public services, and economic growth.

Cold war years back

The Cold War, spanning from the late 1940s to the early 1990s, marked a period of intense geopolitical tension between the United States and its allies in the Western bloc and the Soviet Union and its allies in the Eastern bloc. Characterized by ideological, economic, and military competition, it saw proxy wars, nuclear arms race, and espionage. Key events include the Berlin Airlift, Korean War, Cuban Missile Crisis, and the space race. The Cold War ended with the collapse of the Soviet Union, symbolized by the fall of the Berlin Wall in 1989, leading to a new era in global politics.
