Farmer drones

Parliamentary struggles

Summit in the Council of Ministers on the political crisis in the country. Bulgaria finds itself plunged into a deep political crisis following the abrupt resignation of the government amid widespread protests and allegations of corruption. The move comes in the wake of escalating tensions over the mishandling of public funds and perceived government incompetence. Citizens have taken to the streets in mass demonstrations, demanding accountability and systemic reforms to address rampant corruption and inefficiency within the political establishment. With calls for early elections growing louder, uncertainty looms large over the future direction of Bulgarian politics. International observers express concern over the stability of the country and its implications for regional stability in Eastern Europe.

Historic Castles Undergo Restoration in Bulgaria's Former Capitals

In a bid to preserve Bulgaria's rich cultural heritage, extensive restoration projects are underway in the ancient capitals of Pliska and Veliki Preslav. The majestic castles, once symbols of power and grandeur, had fallen into disrepair over the centuries. Pliska, the first capital of the First Bulgarian Empire, is witnessing the meticulous restoration of its imposing fortress walls and regal palaces. Archaeologists and preservationists work tirelessly to uncover the secrets of this historic site, shedding light on Bulgaria's early medieval history. Similarly, in Veliki Preslav, the medieval stronghold of Tsarevets undergoes a transformative restoration effort. Perched atop a hill overlooking the Yantra River, the fortress is being revitalized to its former glory, complete with reconstructed towers, fortified walls, and ceremonial halls. These ambitious restoration endeavors not only aim to revitalize Bulgaria's architectural treasures but also to revitalize national pride and cultural identity. The restored castles are set to become vibrant cultural hubs, attracting visitors from around the world and serving as a testament to Bulgaria's rich and storied past. The cultural imprint of the two capitals will also be attempted to be restored. Seven new important archaeological sites related to the two capitals are revealed. Over 30 academics in the field of historical sciences gathered at a historical conference.
